R1 EXCEPTION HAZARD:
The v28.1 Round 1 rollback sequence in Steps 10 and 11 was:

    FlattenPositionByName(entryName);
    EmergencyPurgeEntry(entryName, "STOP_AUDIT_MISMATCH");
    return;

If FlattenPositionByName throws (broker disconnect, margin reject, network
timeout, NT8 broker plugin reject), EmergencyPurgeEntry is bypassed. The
local tracking dictionaries remain populated with references to broker
orders that may be in any state. The next bar tick or audit re-amplifies
the mismatch. This re-introduces the exact ghost-order vector R1 was
meant to eliminate.

ROUND-2 FIX:
Wrap the rollback sequence in try-finally:

    try
    {
        FlattenPositionByName(entryName);
    }
    finally
    {
        EmergencyPurgeEntry(entryName, "STOP_AUDIT_MISMATCH");
    }
    return;

This relies on EmergencyPurgeEntry being idempotent and exception-safe,
which it is (Step 12 helper definition: it null-checks every
ConcurrentDictionary, uses TryRemove(key, out _), and wraps the entire
body in a try/catch that logs but does not re-throw).
